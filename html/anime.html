What Is Anime?

The term anime [a·nuh·mei] is a Japanese colloquialism used as an abbreviation for the term “animation.” Generally in Japan, the word anime (anime in Japanese: アニメ) is synonymous with animation of any kind from anywhere. Internationally, however, anime is typically referred to as animation that is produced in Japan. The distinction between anime and cartoons is more cultural than it is technical and its origins date over 100 years ago.

How Anime Is Made

Anime is no different technically to the animation productions of Western culture. Which, if you know anything about, takes an incredible amount of time and work. Stories must be written, storyboarded, workshopped, taken into an animatic, voiced, and animated. This is a process that can take months and often years to finish. All of these projects are created from an entire studio of artists typically led by a director.
